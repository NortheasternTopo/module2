Hokusai, K. (c. 1833). The Waterfall Where Yoshitsune Washed His Horse at Yoshino in Yamato Province [Woodblock print].

In The Waterfall Where Yoshitsune Washed His Horse, Hokusai uses rhythmic curves, flowing lines, and repeated wave patterns to animate the cascading water with a powerful sense of movement. The dynamic composition leads the viewer’s eye through the landscape, capturing both the energy of the waterfall and the ritual stillness of the figures, blending nature’s motion with narrative grace.
