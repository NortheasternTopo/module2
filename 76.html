Unknown. (c. 30,000 BCE). Chauvet Cave Paintings [Pigment on limestone].

In the Chauvet Cave, movement is expressed with remarkable dynamism. Lines are repeated, overlapped, and shifted slightly, creating a sense of animation. Lions, bison, and horses seem to gallop across the wall in a primal sequence of motion. The rhythm is fluid and natural, mimicking the pulse of a stampede or the shifting gaze of a hunter tracking prey.
