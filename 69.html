Khoshbaten, B. (2019). Jameh Mosque of Tabriz (11th–13th century) [Architectural photograph].

The vaulted ceiling of the Jameh Mosque of Tabriz exemplifies the principle of repetition, alignment, and pattern through its intricate brickwork and rhythmic geometry. Repeating arches and tessellated forms create a sense of harmony and infinite depth, uniting structural clarity with spiritual transcendence in a breathtaking orchestration of visual order.
