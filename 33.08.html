Leonardo da Vinci. (c. 1490). Vitruvian Man [Ink on paper].

Leonardo da Vinci’s Vitruvian Man exemplifies the use of geometric harmony and proportion as foundational principles of visual organization. By inscribing the human body within a circle and square, da Vinci illustrates the alignment between natural form and mathematical order—revealing symmetry not as decorative, but as a tool for understanding universal balance.
